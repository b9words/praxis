### **Arena 5: The War Room - 20 Case Study Blueprints**

#### **Core Competency: Crisis Decision-Making**

**1. Title: The Supply Chain Collapse**
*   **Competency:** Crisis Decision-Making
*   **Challenge Type:** The Real-Time Crisis Simulation
*   **Dilemma:** A sudden geopolitical event (e.g., a new tariff, a port closure) has instantly cut off access to the single-source supplier for a critical component in your flagship hardware product. Your entire production line will halt in 72 hours.
*   **Task:** A 60-minute simulation unfolds via a stream of alerts. You receive updates on your dwindling inventory, frantic emails from your Head of Manufacturing, a proposal for an expensive alternate supplier from your Head of Procurement, and demands for a public statement from your Head of PR. You must make a series of time-stamped decisions to mitigate the damage.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Supply Chain Map]` A map showing your dependency on the single-source supplier.
    *   `[Financial Model]` A model showing the daily cash burn from the production stoppage.
    *   `[Alternate Supplier Dossier]` A hasty report on a potential, unvetted, and 3x more expensive alternate supplier.

**2. Title: The Activist Short-Seller Report**
*   **Competency:** Crisis Decision-Making
*   **Challenge Type:** The Leaked Memo Response Chain
*   **Dilemma:** A notorious activist short-selling firm has just published a scathing 100-page report accusing your company of fraud. The report is a mix of legitimate criticisms and wild exaggerations. Your stock is down 30% in pre-market trading.
*   **Task:** **Stage 1:** You are presented with the short-seller's report and the live, crashing stock price. **Stage 2:** Your investor relations and legal Slack channels are in a state of panic. You must issue clear instructions on whether to issue an immediate rebuttal or wait. **Stage 3:** You have one hour to draft the official company response to the allegations before the market opens.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Short-Seller Report]` The full, damning report from the activist.
    *   `[Your Financials]` Your own internal financial documents.
    *   `[Legal Memo]` A memo from your lawyers on the risks of making a hasty, incomplete public statement.

**3. Title: The Hostile Takeover Bid**
*   **Competency:** Crisis Decision-Making
*   **Challenge Type:** The Emergency Board Communication
*   **Dilemma:** Your larger, more aggressive competitor has just launched a surprise, unsolicited "hostile takeover" bid for your company at a 20% premium to your current stock price. They have sent the offer directly to your shareholders.
*   **Task:** You have 90 minutes to prepare the urgent, confidential briefing for your Board of Directors. The memo must analyze the offer, provide a preliminary valuation of whether the bid is fair, and, most importantly, recommend a defensive strategy (e.g., "Just Say No," "Find a 'White Knight' acquirer," "Negotiate for a higher price").
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Takeover Offer]` The official offer letter from the hostile acquirer.
    *   `[Banker's Analysis]` A hastily prepared analysis from your investment banker on the company's standalone value.
    *   `[Board Profiles]` Profiles of your board members, indicating who might be tempted to take the offer.

**4. Title: The Key Person Departure**
*   **Competency:** Crisis Decision-Making
*   **Challenge Type:** The First 100 Days Plan (but in reverse)
*   **Dilemma:** The brilliant, visionary head of your AI division—the public face of your entire future strategy—has just resigned to join your biggest competitor. Her departure creates a massive strategic vacuum and a potential crisis of investor confidence.
*   **Task:** The resignation will be announced in 48 hours. Your task is to create the "Emergency Transition Plan." The plan must detail the immediate actions to be taken, including the internal and external communication strategy, the appointment of an interim leader, and a clear plan to reassure the remaining AI team and prevent a mass exodus of talent.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Resignation Letter]` The departing executive's resignation letter.
    *   `[Org Chart]` The org chart of the AI division she led.
    *   `[Analyst Reports]` A collection of reports highlighting how critical this executive is to the company's story.

**5. Title: The "Going Concern" Warning**
*   **Competency:** Crisis Decision-Making
*   **Challenge Type:** The Redline Negotiation
*   **Dilemma:** Your struggling company is in the final stages of securing a life-saving round of financing. At the last minute, your auditors have informed you that they may have to include a "going concern" warning in your financial statements, which would almost certainly kill the deal and trigger bankruptcy.
*   **Task:** You enter a high-stakes negotiation simulation with the AI-powered "Lead Audit Partner." Your task is to convince him not to issue the warning. You must present a credible, data-driven operating plan that demonstrates a viable path to profitability, and you must negotiate the specific milestones and covenants you are willing to commit to in order to avoid the warning.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Auditor's Memo]` The draft memo from the auditor outlining their concerns.
    *   `[Your Financial Model]` Your own internal, optimistic financial forecast.
    *   `[Financing Term Sheet]` The term sheet for the financing deal, which is contingent on a clean audit.

#### **Core Competency: Cybersecurity & Data Privacy**

**6. Title: The Ransomware Attack**
*   **Competency:** Cybersecurity & Data Privacy
*   **Challenge Type:** The Real-Time Crisis Simulation
*   **Dilemma:** Your company's entire network has been encrypted by a ransomware attack. A bitcoin wallet address is on every screen with a demand for $10 million. Your operations are completely offline.
*   **Task:** A 60-minute simulation unfolds. You are presented with conflicting advice from your Head of Security (who is trying to restore from backups), your General Counsel (who advises that paying may be illegal), and your Head of Sales (who is losing millions every hour). You must make a series of time-stamped decisions, culminating in the final "Pay/Don't Pay" choice.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Ransom Note]` The attacker's note with the demand.
    *   `[Cyber Insurance Policy]` Your cyber insurance policy, detailing the terms of coverage.
    *   `[FBI Advisory]` An official advisory from the FBI strongly discouraging ransom payments.

**7. Title: The Data Breach: Containment & Communication**
*   **Competency:** Cybersecurity & Data Privacy
*   **Challenge Type:** The Leaked Memo Response Chain
*   **Dilemma:** Your security team has just discovered that a hacker has been inside your network for the past six months and has exfiltrated the personal data of millions of customers. The hacker is now threatening to release the data publicly unless you pay them.
*   **Task:** **Stage 1:** You are presented with the forensic report. **Stage 2:** The hacker posts a sample of the stolen data on the dark web, and a journalist contacts you for comment. You must issue immediate instructions to your team. **Stage 3:** You have one hour to draft the official notification letter that will be sent to all affected customers and the press release that will be sent to the media.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Forensic Report]` The technical report from your security team detailing the breach.
    *   `[Legal Memo]` A memo from your lawyers outlining your disclosure obligations under GDPR and other laws.
    *   `[PR Crisis Plan]` Your pre-approved data breach communication plan.

**8. Title: The Insider Threat**
*   **Competency:** Cybersecurity & Data Privacy
*   **Challenge Type:** The Forensic Audit
*   **Dilemma:** Your company's most valuable intellectual property—the source code for your next-generation product—has just shown up on a competitor's new product. You suspect a high-level employee who recently quit to join the competitor is the source of the leak.
*   **Task:** You are the General Counsel. You are given access to a "clean room" data folder containing the departed employee's network access logs, email archive, and building entry/exit records for their final 30 days. Your task is to find the digital "smoking gun" and produce a timeline of events to be used as evidence in a major trade secret lawsuit.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Data Folder]` The folder of employee logs and emails.
    *   `[IP Documents]` Documents detailing the specific trade secrets that were stolen.
    *   `[Competitor's Product Specs]` The public specifications of the competitor's new product.

**9. Title: The Nation-State Actor**
*   **Competency:** Cybersecurity & Data Privacy
*   **Challenge Type:** The Emergency Board Communication
*   **Dilemma:** Your CISO informs you that your network has been breached not by criminals, but by a sophisticated hacking group linked to a foreign government. They have not stolen customer data; instead, they have been subtly altering your product's source code. The motive is unknown but could be industrial espionage or sabotage.
*   **Task:** This is a highly sensitive national security issue. Your task is to write the urgent, confidential briefing for your Board of Directors. The memo must explain the situation, the immense and uncertain risks, and your plan to engage with government agencies like the FBI and CISA.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[CISO's Report]` The highly technical report from your Chief Information Security Officer.
    *   `[Threat Intel Brief]` A brief from a third-party intelligence firm on the suspected government hacking group.
    *   `[Legal Memo]` A memo from your outside counsel on the legal obligations of cooperating with a government investigation.

**10. Title: The "Ethical Hack" Dilemma**
*   **Competency:** Cybersecurity & Data Privacy
*   **Challenge Type:** The Public Statement / Press Release
*   **Dilemma:** A "white hat" security researcher has discovered a critical vulnerability in your product. They have responsibly disclosed it to you but have given you a 90-day deadline to fix it before they publish their findings publicly. The fix is complex and will require delaying a major product launch.
*   **Task:** You have decided to delay the product launch to fix the vulnerability. Your task is to write the public blog post announcing the delay. The challenge is to do so with transparency and humility, thanking the researcher and framing the decision as a sign of your company's commitment to security, turning a potential PR disaster into a brand-building moment.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Researcher's Report]` The full technical report from the security researcher.
    *   `[Engineering Plan]` The revised engineering plan showing the new, delayed launch date.
    *   `[Marketing Plan]` The original marketing plan for the now-delayed launch.

#### **Core Competency: Competitive Warfare**

**11. Title: The Competitor's Gambit**
*   **Competency:** Competitive Warfare
*   **Challenge Type:** The Pre-Mortem Red Team
*   **Dilemma:** Your biggest and most aggressive competitor has just announced a major new strategic initiative that seems brilliant and threatens to upend the market. Your leadership team is in a panic and is proposing a hasty, reactive plan to copy the competitor's strategy.
*   **Task:** You are the Head of Strategy. You believe reacting is a mistake. Your task is to write a Red Team analysis of your *competitor's* new strategy. You must identify the hidden weaknesses, the high costs, and the potential points of failure in their plan, and propose a counter-strategy that exploits those weaknesses instead of copying their strengths.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Competitor's Announcement]` The press release and media coverage of your competitor's new strategy.
    *   `[Your Team's Plan]` The reactive plan proposed by your leadership team.
    *   `[Market Data]` Data on the market that you can use to find flaws in the competitor's logic.

**12. Title: The Disinformation Campaign**
*   **Competency:** Competitive Warfare
*   **Challenge Type:** The Leaked Memo Response Chain
*   **Dilemma:** An anonymous online report filled with false and damaging rumors about your company's product quality and financial health is being aggressively pushed on social media and is starting to be picked up by mainstream journalists. You suspect a competitor is behind the campaign.
*   **Task:** **Stage 1:** You are presented with the fake report and the escalating social media firestorm. **Stage 2:** Your internal communications Slack channel is debating how to respond. You must issue instructions. **Stage 3:** You must draft the official company statement that factually debunks the false claims without giving the story more oxygen or appearing overly defensive.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Fake Report]` The anonymous, false report.
    *   `[Social Media Feed]` The simulated social media feed showing the story spreading.
    *   `[Your Internal Data]` Your own internal data that you can use to prove the claims are false.

**13. Title: The Price War**
*   **Competency:** Competitive Warfare
*   **Challenge Type:** The Real-Time Crisis Simulation
*   **Dilemma:** Your main competitor, in a desperate move to gain market share, has just announced a 50% price cut on their competing product. Your sales team is reporting that deals are stalling and you are about to lose major customers.
*   **Task:** A 60-minute simulation unfolds. You are presented with a live stream of emails from your sales team, a financial model showing the devastating impact of matching the price cut, and proposals from your marketing team for non-price responses (e.g., adding new features, offering better support). You must make a series of time-stamped decisions on how to respond without destroying your company's profitability.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Competitor's Announcement]` The press release announcing the price cut.
    *   `[Financial Model]` A model that allows you to see the impact of different responses on your P&L.
    *   `[Customer Emails]` A stream of emails from customers threatening to leave unless you match the price.

**14. Title: The Poaching Attack**
*   **Competency:** Competitive Warfare
*   **Challenge Type:** The High-Stakes Negotiation
*   **Dilemma:** Your primary competitor has just hired away one of your key VPs. Now, they are systematically targeting her entire 50-person team with aggressive offers. This is not just hiring; it's a deliberate attempt to cripple your organization.
*   **Task:** You enter a negotiation simulation not with the competitor, but with one of your own "at-risk" senior engineers who has a competing offer. The AI-powered "Engineer" will tell you their reasons for considering the move. Your task is to go beyond a simple salary counter-offer and use the opportunity to understand the root causes of the team's dissatisfaction, and make credible promises to fix them.
*   -**Case File Assets:**
    *   `[HR Report]` A report on the competitor's aggressive offers and the list of at-risk employees.
    *   `[Employee Survey]` Past employee survey data that may hint at the underlying issues.
    *   `[Your Counter-Offer Toolkit]` A list of the levers you can pull (salary, equity, promotion, new projects).

**15. Title: The "Sherlock" Scan**
*   **Competency:** Competitive Warfare
*   **Challenge Type:** The S-1 Teardown
*   **Dilemma:** Apple is famous for "Sherlocking"—observing a popular third-party app and then building its core functionality directly into the next version of their operating system, instantly making the app obsolete.
*   **Task:** You are the founder of a successful app that lives on a major tech platform (like iOS or Salesforce). Your task is to analyze the platform owner's recent announcements, patent filings, and developer conference keynotes. You must produce a report that identifies the "early warning signs" that your app is at risk of being "Sherlocked" and proposes a strategic pivot to make your business more defensible.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Platform Announcements]` A collection of recent blog posts and keynote transcripts from the platform owner.
    *   `[Your App's Metrics]` Data on your app's growth and user engagement.
    *   `[Case Studies]` A brief history of other apps that have been "Sherlocked" by this platform in the past.

**16. Title: The Surprise Successor**
*   **Competency:** Crisis Decision-Making
*   **Challenge Type:** The Emergency Board Communication
*   **Dilemma:** You are the CEO of a company. You have just been diagnosed with a serious illness that will require you to take an immediate, six-month leave of absence. You must name an interim CEO, but your two direct reports and likely successors are fierce rivals.
*   **Task:** You have 24 hours to prepare the confidential briefing for your Board of Directors. The memo must recommend one of the two rivals as interim CEO, provide a clear and defensible rationale for your choice, and, most importantly, outline a detailed plan to manage the "losing" executive to ensure they don't quit and destabilize the company in your absence.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Medical Note]` Your doctor's note detailing the need for a leave of absence.
    *   `[Candidate Profiles]` The full dossiers on your two rival direct reports.
    *   `[Org Chart]` The current executive org chart.

**17. Title: The Plant Fire**
*   **Competency:** Crisis Decision-Making
*   **Challenge Type:** The Real-Time Crisis Simulation
*   **Dilemma:** A major fire has just broken out at your company's primary manufacturing facility. Initial reports are chaotic, with reports of injuries and a total production stoppage.
*   **Task:** A 60-minute simulation unfolds. You are presented with a stream of inputs: updates from the fire chief, frantic emails from your COO, demands for information from the local media, and social media posts from worried employee families. You must make a series of time-stamped decisions to manage the immediate crisis.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Initial Incident Report]` The first, chaotic report from the plant manager.
    *   `[Crisis Plan]` The company's official (and likely inadequate) crisis management plan.
    *   `[Financial Model]` A model showing the daily financial impact of the plant being offline.

**18. Title: The FAA Grounding**
*   **Competency:** Crisis Decision-Making
*   **Challenge Type:** The Leaked Memo Response Chain
*   **Dilemma:** You are the CEO of an airline. The FAA has just issued an emergency order to ground your entire fleet of Boeing 737 MAX aircraft due to a safety concern, effective immediately. Your operations are in chaos.
*   **Task:** **Stage 1:** You are presented with the official FAA directive and the ensuing media firestorm. **Stage 2:** An internal memo you wrote months ago downplaying the safety concerns is leaked to the press. You must issue instructions to your team. **Stage 3:** You have one hour to draft the official statement to your customers and the script for the video message you will post on social media.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[FAA Directive]` The official grounding order.
    *   `[Leaked Memo]` The damaging internal memo.
    *   `[Operational Data]` A report showing the massive number of flight cancellations and stranded passengers.

**19. Title: The Rogue Employee**
*   **Competency:** Crisis Decision-Making
*   **Challenge Type:** The Forensic Audit
*   **Dilemma:** A single, high-level trader at your investment bank has just been discovered to have hidden massive losses through a series of unauthorized trades, a "rogue trader" scenario. The total loss is unknown but could threaten the firm's solvency.
*   **Task:** You are the Head of Risk. You are given access to the rogue trader's complete, complex trade blotter for the past month. Your task is to conduct a rapid forensic audit to determine the full, catastrophic extent of the losses and produce a definitive report for the Federal Reserve before the market opens.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Trade Blotter]` The trader's massive, complex list of trades.
    *   `[Risk Alerts]` A series of risk alerts that were ignored by the trader's managers.
    *   `[Internal Emails]` Emails showing the trader lying to his superiors.

**20. Title: The Sudden Death**
*   **Competency:** Crisis Decision-Making
*   **Challenge Type:** The Emergency Board Communication
*   **Dilemma:** You are the Chairman of the Board. You have just been informed that your company's highly effective and beloved CEO has died unexpectedly overnight. The company's succession plan is not yet finalized.
*   **Task:** It is 5:00 AM. You have two hours before the market opens. Your task is to write the complete communications plan. This must include the press release announcing the death and naming an interim CEO, the internal memo to all employees, and the key talking points for the calls you must make to your top five institutional shareholders.
*   **Case File Assets:**
    *   `[Succession Plan]` The company's incomplete succession planning document.
    *   `[Candidate Profiles]` Profiles of the potential internal candidates for interim CEO.
    *   `[Stock Chart]` The company's current stock chart.
